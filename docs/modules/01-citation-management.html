Module 1: Citation Management

Organize your references effectively

🎯 Learning Objectives

After completing this module, you will be able to:

  • Set up and use a citation management tool (Zotero or Paperpile)
  • Organize and sync your reference library
  • Generate citations and bibliographies in any citation style
  • Understand what a Biosketch is and how to create one for grant applications

Overview

This module introduces citation management and two free tools: Zotero and Paperpile. It also covers the Biosketch, a document required for grant applications. Go through the summary texts on this page and review the presentations, recordings, guides, and other resources in the Resources Section to learn more.

✅ Complete the assignment at the end of this module and the associated knowledge check quiz on Canvas to earn credit toward your certificate!

Why Citation Management Matters

Manually tracking hundreds of references is error-prone and time-consuming. Citation management software lets you:

  • Capture references from databases, websites, and PDFs in one click
  • Auto-generate citations and bibliographies in any style (APA, MLA, Chicago, etc.)
  • Sync your library across devices and collaborate with team members
  • Attach and annotate PDFs alongside your references

Zotero vs. Paperpile

Choosing the Right Tool

Both tools are free to start and cover similar core workflow. Review the presentation slides and recordings in the Resources Section to learn more.

Price Free (300 MB storage) UCR Subscription using UCR email address
Best for Word, LibreOffice, standalone use Google Docs users
Browser extension ✅ All browsers ✅ Google Chrome
Collaboration ✅ Group libraries ✅ Shared folders
Mobile app ✅ ✅
PDF annotation ✅ Built-in ✅ Built-in

Zotero

  • Download at zotero.org — freemium (subscription comes with more storage but the features are the same), open source
  • Install the Zotero Connector browser extension to save references in one click
  • Integrates with Microsoft Word, LibreOffice, and Google Docs
  • Sync your library across devices with a free Zotero account

Paperpile

  • UCR Subscription using UCR email address
  • Works entirely in the Google Docs environment — no separate app needed
  • Install the browser extension and Google Docs add-on
  • Best choice if your entire workflow lives in Google Drive

Biosketches for Grant Applications

A biosketch is a short professional biography required by federal funders (NIH, NSF) as part of grant applications. It summarizes your education, positions, contributions to science, and research support.

  • NIH and NSF each have slightly different biosketch formats
  • The UCR Library guide walks you through each section step by step
  • Creating your biosketch early before grant deadlines saves significant time
