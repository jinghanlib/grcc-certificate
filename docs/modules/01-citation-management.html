Module 1: Citation Management

Organize your references effectively

🎯 Learning Objectives

After completing this module, you will be able to:

  • Set up and use a citation management tool (Zotero or Paperpile)
  • Organize and sync your reference library
  • Generate citations and bibliographies in any citation style
  • Understand what a Biosketch is and how to create one for grant applications

Overview

This module introduces citation management and two tools: Zotero and Paperpile. It also covers the Biosketch, a document required for federal grant applications.

✅ Complete the assignment at the end of this module and the associated knowledge check quiz on Canvas to earn credit toward your badge.

Why Citation Management Matters

Manually tracking hundreds of references is error-prone and time-consuming. Citation management software lets you:

  • Capture references from databases, websites, and PDFs in one click
  • Auto-generate citations and bibliographies in any style (APA, MLA, Chicago, etc.)
  • Sync your library across devices and collaborate with team members
  • Attach and annotate PDFs alongside your references

See the UCR Library Citation Management Guide for an overview of all tools available to UCR students.

Zotero

Zotero is free and open source, works with Microsoft Word, LibreOffice, and Google Docs, and lets you sync your library across devices.

Getting started:

  1. Download Zotero at zotero.org
  2. Install the Zotero Connector browser extension to save references in one click
  3. Create a free Zotero account to sync across devices
  4. See the UCR Citation Management Guide for style-specific tips

Paperpile

Paperpile is available free to UCR students using your UCR email address. It lives entirely inside Google Docs — no separate desktop app needed.

Getting started:

  1. Sign up at paperpile.com using your UCR email
  2. Install the Paperpile browser extension (Chrome) and the Google Docs add-on
  3. Import references directly from Google Scholar, PubMed, or by uploading PDFs

Paperpile now also supports Microsoft Word — install the Paperpile Word add-in if Word is your preferred writing environment.

See the UCR Paperpile LibGuide for step-by-step instructions and tips.

Citing Sources

Once your library is set up, both tools let you insert citations and auto-generate a bibliography in any style. For style-specific guidance (APA, MLA, Chicago, and more), see the UCR How to Cite Sources Guide.

Biosketches for Grant Applications

A biosketch is a short professional biography required by federal funders (NIH, NSF) as part of grant applications. It summarizes your education, positions, contributions to science, and research support.

  • NIH and NSF each have slightly different biosketch formats
  • The UCR Library Biosketch Guide walks you through each section step by step
  • Creating your biosketch early — before grant deadlines — saves significant time
